How to read this page
Each card lists (1) a common failure surface and (2) the claim-safe structural effect. Illustrative only.
FX

Financial markets & trading infrastructure

Gateways, routing, risk checks

  • Failure: tail spikes, retry storms, queue avalanches during volatility.
  • Effect: bounds worst-case behavior; prevents sustained escalation beyond origin.
Illustrative: local disturbance → bounded region → stable remainder.
CL

Cloud platforms & SRE-critical services

Control planes, multi-tenant services

  • Failure: thundering herds, brownouts, overload feedback loops.
  • Effect: precludes unbounded amplification; keeps failures local and non-sustaining.
Illustrative: overload loop → bounded response → recovery without oscillation.
TX

Telecom & real-time networks

VoIP cores, signaling, edge

  • Failure: signaling storms, synchronized retries, congestion cascades.
  • Effect: prevents propagation across network domains; reduces blast radius under stress.
Illustrative: storm origin → contained segment → protected neighbors.
EN

Energy & utilities

Grid control, distributed assets

  • Failure: unstable recovery, oscillatory control interaction, cascade triggers.
  • Effect: bounded trajectories and non-propagating instability categories (structural).
Illustrative: disturbance → bounded actuation envelope → stable recovery.
AV

Aviation, transport & logistics systems

Scheduling, routing, control loops

  • Failure: cascading delays, backlog amplification, brittle recovery sequences.
  • Effect: prevents delay cascades from sustaining system-wide; supports stable recovery dynamics.
Illustrative: local disruption → bounded backlog → controlled throughput.
SC

Safety-critical & verification-heavy systems

Determinism for assurance

  • Failure: uncontrolled escalation paths that defeat assurance cases.
  • Effect: deterministic constraints; repeatable behavior suitable for verification boundaries.
Illustrative: constrained evolution → predictable envelope → stronger assurance.
MF

Manufacturing & industrial automation

Lines, PLC-adjacent orchestration

  • Failure: local faults triggering chain stoppages; oscillatory restart loops.
  • Effect: contains the disturbance; avoids unbounded restart amplification.
Illustrative: fault cell → bounded restart regime → protected upstream/downstream.
HC

Healthcare infrastructure & devices

Critical workflows, deterministic operation

  • Failure: overload during peak events; cascading service degradation.
  • Effect: structural bounding and containment; reduces systemic risk under disturbances.
Illustrative: surge → bounded behavior → continuity of critical paths.
EC

E-commerce & payment flows

Checkout, fraud gates, orchestration

  • Failure: cascading timeouts, retries across microservices, queue blow-ups.
  • Effect: prevents cross-service escalation from sustaining; keeps issues localized.
Illustrative: timeout cascade → bounded response → stable degradation.
RB

Autonomous & robotic systems

Actuation under uncertainty

  • Failure: unsafe escalation due to uncertainty accumulation in decision loops.
  • Effect: bounded trajectories; constrains worst-case actuation behavior structurally.
Illustrative: uncertainty ↑ → constrained authority → safe envelope.
